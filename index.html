What Are Peptides & Why They Matter

Peptides are short chains of amino acids—the building blocks of proteins. Smaller than proteins, peptides play key roles in signaling and regulating biological processes. Researchers rely on peptides to:

  • Understand complex cellular functions
  • Develop new therapeutic agents
  • Study hormonal regulation and cell communication

Their precision and ability to mimic natural pathways make them ideal for advanced scientific research.
